## Overview

This opinion piece, published in ASA Generations, offered a young adult's perspective on how older adults are portrayed in media and advertising, examining harmful stereotypes and proposing ways to improve age representation in content.

## Contribution

- Provided unique intergenerational perspective on ageism in media
- Identified specific examples of harmful aging stereotypes in advertising
- Proposed actionable recommendations for age-friendly media
- Connected personal caregiving experiences to media critique
- Contributed to Ageism Awareness Day discussions

## Process and methods

- Analyzed media portrayals and advertising content
- Drew on personal experiences with older adults and caregiving
- Reviewed Associated Press style guidance on age-inclusive language
- Synthesized observations into accessible opinion piece
- Collaborated with ASA Generations editorial team

## Impact

The article was published in conjunction with Ageism Awareness Day and contributed to ongoing discussions about age-inclusive media representation, reaching aging advocates, media professionals, and the broader public health community.

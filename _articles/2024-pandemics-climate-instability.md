## Overview

This news article explores the relationship between climate change and the increasing frequency of pandemics. By examining research on zoonotic diseases, deforestation, and environmental disruption, the piece highlights how human activities and climate instability contribute to disease emergence and spread.

## Contribution

- Synthesized current research on climate change and pandemic emergence
- Connected environmental factors to disease transmission patterns
- Highlighted the role of deforestation and urbanization in zoonotic disease spread
- Provided accessible explanation of complex climate-health relationships

## Process and methods

- Reviewed recent peer-reviewed research on climate and infectious diseases
- Analyzed NETEC reports on vector-borne diseases
- Synthesized data from Oxford University on deforestation trends
- Interviewed concepts from The Lancet and other medical journals
- Drafted article for NYU GPH Journal of Public Health Disasters

## Impact

The article contributed to public discourse on climate change and public health, providing evidence-based insights for students, researchers, and health professionals studying disaster preparedness and environmental health connections.

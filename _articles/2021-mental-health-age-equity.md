## Overview

This opinion piece examined workplace age discrimination and mental health impacts, drawing on the documentary "Duty Free" to propose concrete improvements for creating age-equitable workplaces that value employees throughout their careers.

## Contribution

- Connected workplace ageism to mental health and wellbeing
- Highlighted challenges faced by both older and younger workers in job searches
- Introduced concept of "last page of handbook" for employee protections
- Proposed actionable recommendations for employers and employees
- Emphasized importance of multigenerational workforce

## Process and methods

- Analyzed "Duty Free" documentary about workplace ageism
- Reviewed Age Equity Alliance resources on multigenerational workforce
- Synthesized research on job market discrimination
- Drew on personal observations and experiences
- Developed accessible recommendations for workplace equity

## Impact

The article contributed to Age Equity Alliance's advocacy for workplace age equity, providing practical guidance for both employers and employees on creating more equitable and supportive work environments.

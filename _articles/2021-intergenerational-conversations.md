## Overview

This personal essay explored intergenerational connections and ageism in the digital age, drawing on personal experiences with caregiving and gerontology studies to advocate for more inclusive, age-diverse workplaces and communities.

## Contribution

- Shared personal journey into gerontology and elder care
- Challenged stereotypes about older adults and technology
- Connected pandemic isolation to ageism and intergenerational conflict
- Advocated for intergenerational workplace models
- Provided youth perspective on combating ageism

## Process and methods

- Drew on personal caregiving experiences
- Referenced research on social isolation and cognitive function
- Analyzed media representations of intergenerational conflict
- Reviewed Age Equity Alliance resources on workplace inclusion
- Synthesized academic and personal perspectives

## Impact

The article contributed to Age Equity Alliance's mission of promoting intergenerational understanding and challenged stereotypes about older adults' technology use and workplace adaptability.
